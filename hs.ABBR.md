## BE
Big endian. In the BE mode, individual bytes (octets) that make up a single data value are transmitted (or stored) in the LSB order - the least significant byte comes first, as opposed to the LE (little endian) mode, where the most significant byte comes first. Therefore, LE seems "reversed", while BE reads as expected. The `CR LF` sequence that marks the end of line on Windows is `0D 0A` in BE, and `0A 0D` in LE.
